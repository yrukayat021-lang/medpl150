Illustrated Environment

For this project I selected an environmental photograph of an urban street scene. I chose this setting because the strong architectural shapes and perspective allowed me to experiment with simplifying real-world forms into clean vector illustrations. Using Adobe Illustrator, I placed the photograph on its own locked layer and used it as a template while recreating the environment with vector shapes and paths. I divided the illustration into foreground, middle ground, and background layers to organize the composition and maintain depth.

The illustration was limited to a three-color palette that I generated using Adobe Color to keep the design visually cohesive. Rather than using Image Trace, I manually redrew the buildings, street elements, and environmental features using tools such as the Pen Tool, Shape Builder, and basic geometric shapes. This approach allowed me to transform the photographic environment into a simplified graphic style while still preserving the structure and atmosphere of the original setting.
